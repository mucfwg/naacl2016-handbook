Abstract#==#In this paper, we challenge a basic assumption of many cross-lingual transfer
techniques: the availability of word aligned parallel corpora, and consider
ways to accommodate situations in which such resources do not exist. We show
experimentally that, here again, weakly supervised cross-lingual learning
techniques can prove useful, once adapted to transfer knowledge across pairs of
languages.
