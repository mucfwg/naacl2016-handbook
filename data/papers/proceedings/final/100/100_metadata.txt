Abstract#==#In this work, we present a novel counter-fitting method which injects antonymy
and synonymy constraints into vector space representations in order to improve
the vectors' capability for judging semantic similarity. Applying this method
to publicly available pre-trained word vectors leads to a new state of the art
performance on the SimLex-999 dataset. We also show how the method can be used
to tailor the word vector space for the downstream task of dialogue state
tracking, resulting in robust improvements across different dialogue domains.
