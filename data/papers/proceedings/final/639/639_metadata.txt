Abstract#==#We introduce the first dataset for sequential
vision-to-language, and explore how this data
may be used for the task of visual storytelling.
The dataset includes 81,743 unique photos
in 20,211 sequences, aligned to descriptive
and story language. We establish several
strong baselines for the storytelling task, and
motivate an automatic metric to benchmark
progress. We argue that modelling figurative
and social language, as provided for in this
data and the storytelling task, has the potential to move artificial
intelligence from basic understandings of typical visual scenes towards more
and more human-like understanding of grounded event structure and subjective
expression.
